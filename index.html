datap in 10 seconds

Define all your data needs for your R project in a single yaml file. Wherever the source. Whatever the format.

Use the datap API to load your data into your R session.

Share your datap contexts, add them to your source code repository, or keep various versions for development and production.

datap in 30 seconds

Within your datap context (i.e. the datap yaml file), you can

  1. define data processing pipes:
    1. Where is your data coming from? (internet, file, db, memory, etc.)
    2. How should your data be pre-processed? (cleaning, plausibility checking, missing value handling, etc.)
  2. define taps by
    1. giving short id's to your datasets
    2. organise your datasets into hierarchies

Once you defined your datap context, you can call your taps from R 1. by accessing a single, consistent API 4. to load your data into an R session
